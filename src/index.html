What is GAN?

Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) are a class of deep learning models, known for their remarkable ability to generate synthetic images that look like natural images. A GAN internally combines two neural networks, called generator and discriminator, to play a game where the generator creates "fake" data and the discriminator guesses whether that data is real or fake. A perfect GAN is one that generates fake data that is virtually indistinguishable from real data. For a more detailed introduction to GANs, check out the original paper by Goodfellow et al., or google it --- you'll find many introductory articles.

What is this tool for?

GANs are very difficult to train and for people to understand. The iterative, dynamic interplay between the generator and discriminator is challenging for novices to recognize, and sometimes even for experts to fully understand. In response, we developed GAN Lab, a novel interactive visualization tool for learning and experimenting with GANs. Similar to TensorFlow Playground, here you can learn GANs by interactively training GAN models for 2D data distributions and visualizing their inner-workings.

How can I play with GAN Lab?

You can start by choosing one of the 2D data distributions on the top menu and click the play button. You'll see animated visualization as the training progresses. Ideally, the distribution of the purple dots (fake data) will become indistinguishable from that of the green dots (real data).

Check out this demo video for more detailed use cases! You can simply play the video or click one of the following sections if you want to jump into.

  • Introduction of GAN Lab (0:00-0:38)
  • Training for a simple distribution with dynamic hyperparameter changes (0:38-1:05)
  • Training of a user-defined complex distribution with manifold visualization (1:05-1:51)
  • Slow-motion mode for learning training steps (1:51-2:19)
  • Manual step-by-step execution for understanding interplay between discriminator & generator (2:19-3:10)

Cool. How is it implemented?

GAN Lab uses TensorFlow.js, an in-browser GPU-accelerated deep learning library. All the model training and visualization are implemented with JavaScript, thus they fully run in your browser. You don't need to install any libraries --- you only need a web browser like Chrome. This significantly broadens people's access to interactive tools for deep learning. The source code is available in GitHub.

Who developed GAN Lab?

GAN Lab is a research project conducted by researchers at Georgia Tech and Google Brain. The project was started while Minsuk Kahng was an intern at Google Big Picture team and Google PAIR.
